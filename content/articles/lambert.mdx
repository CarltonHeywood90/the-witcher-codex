# Lambert: An Archival Study

If Vesemir is the honor of the Wolf School and Geralt is its destiny, **Lambert** is its raw, unvarnished anger. The youngest witcher to be trained within the freezing halls of Kaer Morhen, Lambert stands out for his corrosive cynicism, abrasive tongue, and deep-seated resentment toward the very existence of his profession. Free of any romanticized notions regarding monster hunting, he views his mutations not as a solemn calling or a tragic destiny, but as a cruel, state-sanctioned curse imposed upon him without his consent.

## A Chosen Agony

Lambert’s path to the Wolf School was born of a toxic family dynamic. His father was a violent, abusive alcoholic who routinely terrorized his family. One evening, after wandering into a wilderness swamp while intoxicated, the father was saved from certain death by a passing witcher. Invoking the absolute decree of the **Law of Surprise**, the monster hunter claimed the first thing the father saw upon returning home: his young son, Lambert.

Dragged away to Kaer Morhen, Lambert was subjected to the horrific chemical tortures of the Trial of the Grasses. Watching his childhood peers die screaming on the laboratory slabs left him deeply traumatized, imprinting a permanent hatred for the old mages who designed the process. Unlike Geralt, who accepts his mutation as a tool of survival, Lambert never forgave the universe for robbing him of a normal human life to turn him into a specialized killer-for-hire.

> "You want a ballad about honor, Jaskier? Write about the kids who choked on their own vomit on the stone slabs in the basement down there. Write about how we get paid three coppers to kill a drowner that could rip our lungs out. That's your poetry."
> — Overheard in the Kaer Morhen laboratory

## The Prickly Brother

Lambert's social interactions are defined by a combative, defensive exterior. He routinely mocks Geralt's philosophical entanglements with kings and sorceresses, prefers raw tavern brawls to diplomatic negotiations, and takes twisted pleasure in breaking the elite etiquette of high society.

Yet, beneath this jagged armor lies a deeply loyal brother-in-arms. When Ciri arrived at Kaer Morhen, Lambert channeled his bitter experiences into her training, aggressively preparing her for the harsh reality of the trail. His rough demeanor serves as a psychological defense mechanism; he pushes people away precisely because he understands how quickly those he loves can be snuffed out by a monster’s claws or a human's hatred.

## Combat Mechanics

Lambert’s fighting style reflects his personality—vicious, efficient, and completely devoid of theatrical flourish:

* **Aggressive CQC Swordsmanship:** Specializes in tight, high-pressure close-quarters combat, utilizing heavy parries, brutal elbow strikes, and low-line blade cuts.
* **Combat Sign Inversion:** Exceptionally fast at casting kinetic signs like *Aard* and defensive barriers like *Quen* mid-stride to shatter an enemy's rhythm.
* **Underworld Navigation:** Highly adept at operating within urban slums, tracking targets through back alleys, and securing contracts from the most untrustworthy margins of society.

Lambert remains a vital, contrasting element of the witcher family—a scarred survivor whose sharp tongue hides a fierce commitment to protecting the few brothers he has left.
