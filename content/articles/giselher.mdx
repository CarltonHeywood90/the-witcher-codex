# Giselher: An Archival Study

Every band of desperate rogues requires an anchor to keep their shared fury focused, and for the highwaymen of Geso, that anchor was **Giselher**. A former military conscript who deserted from his regiment during the height of the Nilfgaardian invasions, Giselher is the tactical core and recognized leader of the Rats. He is not a typical, bloodthirsty bandit chief driven by grand greed; he is a pragmatic, profoundly cynical survivor who understands that in a world torn apart by empires, young people must either band together like a pack of wolves or be crushed individually beneath the boots of the infantry.

## The Code of the Deserter

Under Giselher's leadership, the Rats operated with a highly specific, survivalist philosophy. Having witnessed the hollow promises of kings and the brutal reality of military service, he completely rejected state laws, borders, and traditional morality. He established a strict internal code within the gang: absolute mutual loyalty, equal distribution of all stolen loot, and an unyielding commitment to living entirely in the present moment. 

Giselher recognized that the gang's survival depended entirely on their reputation among the local peasantry. He orchestrated a brilliant Robin Hood-style survival strategy, regularly distributing a portion of their stolen gold and luxury goods to the impoverished villages of the borderlands. In exchange, the local populations provided the Rats with vital scout intelligence, secure safehouses, and complete silence when imperial prefects or bounty hunters came searching for their trail.

> "Don't talk to me about justice, girl. I've seen men die for a scrap of silk on a general's banner. The only thing that matters is that we eat tonight, we sleep warm, and we look out for each other. Everything else is a lie told by old men to get young men killed."
> — Confrontation with a newly arrived Ciri

## The Indulgent Descent

Despite his protective instincts, Giselher's leadership was deeply flawed by a reckless, hedonistic fatalism. Knowing that their lives would likely be short and violent, he encouraged the gang to spend their stolen wealth immediately on flamboyant silk clothes, expensive perfumes, fine horses, and massive, drug-fueled parties in local taverns. 

This short-sighted pursuit of indulgence ultimately left the Rats completely unprepared for the clinical, unrelenting tracking methods of Leo Bonhart. When the bounty hunter finally pinned them down in the village of Jealousy, Giselher fought with the desperate, ferocious bravery of a cornered soldier, but his standard battlefield instincts were completely outmatched by the unnatural speed and calculated cruelty of the Witcher Killer.

## Arcane Profiling & Assets

Giselher operates as a veteran small-unit commander and skirmish tactician:

* **Guerrilla Command Mechanics:** Highly proficient in organizing lightning-fast cavalry ambushes, managing hidden supply trails, and maintaining absolute operational security across hostile border provinces.
* **Domestic Intelligence Sourcing:** Expert at manipulating local peasant populations to secure early warnings regarding military movements and tracking units.
* **Military Sabotage and Field Craft:** Utilizes formal military training to identify weak points in merchant caravans and exploit structural vulnerabilities in local provincial garrisons.
