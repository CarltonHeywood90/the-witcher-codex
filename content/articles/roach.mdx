# Roach: An Archival Study

To the outside observer, **Roach** is a simple, serviceable horse—a bay or chestnut mare—carrying a Witcher’s gear along the muddy trails of the North. To Geralt of Rivia, Roach is not a single animal, but a tradition. Geralt has always named every horse he owns "Roach" (from the common freshwater fish, not the insect). Throughout his century-long career, he has cycled through numerous mounts, each serving for a season or a decade before falling to the hazards of the Path, yet each retaining the same name, a silent testament to the Witcher’s grim acceptance of loss.

## The Tradition of the Name

Geralt’s insistence on the name Roach serves as a deliberate emotional distancing mechanism. By refusing to assign unique identities to his mounts, he attempts to minimize the inevitable grief that follows a creature's death in a profession as lethal as his. Most of his Roaches have been mares; Geralt has frequently remarked on his preference for their temperament and reliability over the often more volatile nature of stallions, though he has occasionally made exceptions when circumstances forced his hand.

> "All of my horses are named Roach. You know that very well. It is easier to keep track of the gear if I don't have to change the name on the inventory every time a beast decides to kick the bucket in the middle of a contract."
> — Geralt of Rivia

## Variability of the Breed

While the name is static, the animal is not. Throughout the saga, Roach’s appearance has shifted from bay to chestnut to black, depending on the specific era of Geralt’s journey and the availability of horses in the region. There is no magic behind the name—no reincarnation, no demonic pact—only the stoic, practical nature of a professional who knows that on the Path, everything is temporary, and the only thing that truly lasts is the name itself.

## Tactical & Asset Profile

Roach acts as the primary logistical support for a Witcher’s operation:

* **Pack-Logistics Management:** Expertly handles the weight of specialized silver and steel blades, alchemical saddlebags, and heavy monster trophies without panicking.
* **Adaptable Terrain Navigation:** Trained to traverse the treacherous, monster-infested shortcuts that traditional merchant horses would refuse to approach.
* **Standardized Command Interface:** The Witcher’s training techniques are uniform across every mount that bears the name, ensuring that Geralt never has to relearn how to handle his primary transport when a new Roach arrives.
