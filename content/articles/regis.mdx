# Emiel Regis: An Archival Study

To the untrained eye, **Emiel Regis** appears to be nothing more than an exceptionally articulate, eccentric intellectual operating a modest barber-surgeon practice in Dillingen. He smells of dried herbs, mandrake root, and distilled spirits. However, beneath this unassuming, middle-aged facade lies one of the most powerful entities walking The Continent: a centuries-old **Higher Vampire** whose depth of wisdom is matched only by his absolute mastery over his own primal, predatory instincts.

## The Nature of the Beast

Unlike lesser post-Conjunction monsters, Higher Vampires are completely distinct, highly evolved apex entities. They do not require blood to survive; rather, to them, human blood acts as a highly potent, intoxicating alcohol. 

In his youth, Regis surrendered completely to this addiction, roving the land in a state of perpetual, blood-fueled debauchery. His reckless behavior culminated in an incident where furious local peasants decapitated him, pierced his heart, and buried him alive. Because Higher Vampires possess unparalleled regenerative capabilities, Regis survived—but the excruciating process of physical reconstitution took nearly half a century. This prolonged isolation triggered a deep moral awakening, prompting him to take an absolute, unbreakable vow of blood-sobriety.

> "I am a monster by definition, Master Witcher. Yet, I have spent more years fighting my own nature than your kind has spent fighting the beasts of the wild. Who, then, is truly civilized?"
> — Transcribed from campfire discussions near Chotla

## The Intellectual Anchor of the Hanza

Regis integrated into Geralt’s rescue party during their trek through the southern territories. Initially viewed with extreme suspicion by the Witcher—whose medallion hummed violently in the vampire's presence—Regis quickly rendered himself indispensable. 

His encyclopedic knowledge of medicine, botany, philosophy, and regional geography provided the Hanza with critical strategic advantages. More importantly, Regis became Geralt's primary intellectual confidant. While the rest of the world viewed the Witcher as a mutated tool or a legendary savior, Regis engaged with him as a peer, offering detached, philosophical insights that routinely kept Geralt's bubbling despair from consuming him.

## Capabilities and Arcane Biology

When forced to shed his human camouflage, Regis commands terrifying combat efficiency:

* **Complete Invisibility:** Capable of fading from the sensory perception of both humans and witcher medallions at will.
* **Intangible Phasing:** Can dissolve his physical mass into a cloud of smoke or a massive, bat-like monstrosity to bypass physical barriers.
* **Immunity to Conventional Harm:** Standard steel, silver, fire, and basic magical signs inflict zero permanent damage to his tissue.

Regis represents the ultimate paradox of the Continent: a creature born of literal darkness who displays more genuine empathy, self-control, and profound humanity than the vast majority of the mortal kings currently waging war across the world.
