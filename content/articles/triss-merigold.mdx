# Triss Merigold: An Archival Study

While the Lodge of Sorceresses is largely comprised of cynical, power-hungry cynics, **Triss Merigold** of Maribor historically stood as its conflicted conscience. Exceptionally young for a master sorceress, Triss is renowned for her brilliant chestnut-red hair, her expertise in magical pharmacology, and a rare capacity for genuine empathy. Her life is defined by a fierce internal tug-of-war: her loyalty to her sisterhood, her deep-seated trauma from the battlefields of the First Nilfgaardian War, and her intense, complicated romantic history with Geralt of Rivia.

## The Fourteenth of the Hill

Triss’s idealistic worldview was shattered at the **Battle of Sodden Hill**. Standing on the frontline beside Yennefer to halt the southern expansion, Triss was caught in a devastating imperial magical bombardment. The raw trauma was absolute: she was severely burned, lost her hair, and her chest was crushed so badly that she was originally misidentified among the dead. 

When the dust settled, she was commemorated as the "Fourteenth of the Hill" on the monument erected to honor the fallen mages. Though she survived through extensive skin-grafting potions and reconstructive transmutations, the mental scars remained permanent. The experience left her with a severe form of post-traumatic stress and a deep physical aversion to showing her chest, choosing instead to wear high-collared, modest clothing—a stark contrast to the plunging necklines favored by her peers.

> "They threw fire at us until the earth melted. I don't remember the victory, Jaskier. I only remember the smell of my own skin burning, and the names of the thirteen who didn't get back up."
> — Conversation during a journey to Kaer Morhen

## The Sisterhood's Puppet

Triss’s fatal flaw was her deep emotional dependency on older, more dominant sorceresses—particularly Philippa Eilhart and Yennefer. This vulnerability led her to assist in the founding of the Lodge, where she allowed herself to be swept along by Philippa's cold political machinations. Despite her deep affection for Ciri, whom she had treated like a younger sister during a winter hideout at Kaer Morhen, Triss initially voted with the Lodge to treat the young girl as a political breeding asset to anchor a magical matriarchy.

However, Triss's fundamental goodness ultimately prevailed. As the northern kings began unleashing savage witch hunts across the Continent, she broke away from the shadow games of the Lodge to take direct action. She organized a massive, underground underground railroad network in the city of Novigrad, risking her life on the frontlines to smuggle hunted mages, alchemists, and non-humans out of the reach of King Radovid's executioners.

## Arcane Profiling & Assets

Triss operates with a highly versatile, defensive, and alchemical-focused combat profile:

* **Advanced Pyrotechnic Channelling:** Capable of weaponizing localized fire storms, specifically calling down meteoric elemental strikes (such as *Merigold's Hailstorm*) to shatter advancing infantry columns.
* **Alchemical Mastery:** Highly skilled in the creation of combat elixirs, antitoxins, and tissue-regeneration poultices, serving as a vital battlefield triage asset.
* **Paradoxical Immunity:** Despite being an expert healer and alchemist, Triss is severely allergic to magical potions herself, forcing her to rely entirely on standard herbal remedies when injured.
